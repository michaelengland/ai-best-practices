# AI Academy — Pitch Deck Slide Design

> **Next step:** Use **/deckwright:present** to generate the .pptx file.

## Visual Direction

Inherited from the main deck — same family, same system.

- **Color palette**: Dark bg `0F0F1A` (60%) / text `E0E4E8` + muted `94A3B8` (30%) / teal accent `00B4D8` (10%)
- **Typography**: Georgia (headings) + Calibri (body). Hero 72-120pt, Headline 44pt, Support 28pt, Sources 11pt
- **Visual style**: Billboard — 3-second test, 15 words max, 50%+ whitespace. Radial glow on dark slides.

## Slide Plan

### Slide 1: Title
**Layout**: Title slide
**Content**: "AI Academy" / "From Explorer to Orchestrator in two sessions"
**Speaker notes**: Brief. Let the title sit. ~10 seconds.

### Slide 2: The Hook — "It's slop."
**Layout**: Hero quote (48pt Calibri) + attribution sub (22pt, muted)
**Content**: "\"It's slop.\"" / — Co-founder of OpenAI, October 2025
**Speaker notes**: "The co-founder of the company that made ChatGPT called AI tools slop. That was October 2025." Let it sit. ~10 seconds.
**Source**: Dwarkesh Podcast, October 2025

### Slide 3: The Hook — "This is a skill issue."
**Layout**: Hero quote (32pt Calibri) + attribution sub (22pt, muted)
**Content**: "\"I've never felt this much behind.\nThis is a skill issue.\"" / — Same person. Two months later.
**Speaker notes**: "Two months later — same person — 'This is a skill issue.' If the co-founder of OpenAI has a skill gap, what about our team?" ~15 seconds.
**Source**: Karpathy on X, December 26, 2025

### Slide 4: The Pivot
**Layout**: Single message (44pt hero)
**Content**: "What if your whole team knew the trick?"
**Speaker notes**: Shift from the Karpathy skill gap to organizational opportunity. Pause. ~10 seconds.

### Slide 4: The Stats
**Layout**: Big number + support (two stats combined)
**Content**: "1 billion+" / "use AI tools monthly. Less than 2% use them well."
**Speaker notes**: Source: DataReportal 2026, Reuters. Mass adoption but shallow depth. ~15 seconds.
**Source**: DataReportal 2026, Reuters

### Slide 5: The Twist
**Layout**: Big number (red)
**Content**: "19% longer" / "when you rely on AI without understanding it"
**Speaker notes**: Source: Addy Osmani. Developers who felt faster actually took longer. The gap isn't just unused potential — it's active harm. ~15 seconds.
**Source**: Addy Osmani

### Slide 6: The Gap
**Layout**: Single message (44pt hero)
**Content**: "The gap between using AI and using AI well is massive."
**Speaker notes**: Let this land. The audience should be thinking "that's us." ~10 seconds.

### Slide 7: The Spectrum
**Layout**: Diagram — 7 persona boxes (reuse from main deck)
**Content**: Skeptic → Explorer → Whisperer → Strategist → Operator → Orchestrator → Builder. "Most of us are here." (Explorer) / "The AI Academy takes you here." (Orchestrator)
**Speaker notes**: Same spectrum as the main deck. Self-identification moment. ~20 seconds.

### Slide 8: The Academy
**Layout**: Single message (44pt hero) + support
**Content**: "Two sessions.\nOne hour each." / "Bring your laptop."
**Speaker notes**: The sell starts here. Emphasize: practical, time-bound, low-commitment. ~15 seconds.

### Slide 9: The Curriculum
**Layout**: Two rows (steps layout)
**Content**:
- Workshop 1: Explorer → Strategist — "From vague prompts to a persistent AI playbook"
- Workshop 2: Strategist → Orchestrator — "From doing the work to directing AI teams"
**Speaker notes**: Walk through each briefly. Each one-liner should create curiosity. ~30 seconds.

### Slide 10: CTA
**Layout**: Single message + support
**Content**: "Bring your laptop and a task you do every week."
**Speaker notes**: Specific, low-commitment ask. "Not a task you want to learn — a task you already do. You'll transform it live." ~15 seconds.
